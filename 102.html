Money Can’t Buy Happiness...But Your Credit Score May Help You Find Love!

Mainstreet, X.X. Plenty of dating apps like Tinder have their reputations for offering a lot of one-and-done style of dates…

But over the past half-decade, the entire world & spectrum of online-dating has boomed!

Now over a third of Americans report having looked for love in the virtual world, according to a new PEW survey. And more than half say relationships that begin on a dating site or app are just as successful as those that begin in person.

So it’s no wonder that some of the “casualness” of the early days is starting to give way to more serious considerations on the part of users…

It’s no longer love-at-first-sight.

It’s love-at-first-overview.

“It used to be that I’d see a cute guy & hoped he’d buy me a drink. Now, instead of the awkwardness, I can know a lot about someone before I even meet them”, said Madeline - a young woman who works in finance.

But, there’s one ‘category’ that she says she wished was included...

“...And that’s why I decided to just start asking. I know it can be awkward, but I’m looking for a future. I’d rather be uncomfortable now, than a year down the road!...” 1

Of course, Madeline is talking about Credit Scores.

We get it! It seems unromantic to say the least…

But there’s a good reason for potential mates to be cautious of one another's credit standing, especially when thinking about the future.

After a recent poll, CCN’s Blake Ellis reports:

“Respondents said they worry that a partner with bad credit could hurt their prospects for qualifying for home loans, auto loans or lower interest rates and they'd be irresponsible about handling joint finances.” 2

Now, we know there’s (almost) nothing more awkward than talking about finances.

That’s why our team has gathered & sorted the best advice out there so that YOU can go into your next digital-encounter with confidence...

HERE’S OUR TOP 3 TAKE-AWAYS:

(ONE) - TRUSTWORTHINESS

Step one of any online encounter begins with trust . Heck, it’s the reason that platforms were designed the way they are - to avoid the vagueness (& creepiness) too many people get from that first-date experience.

The only trouble is - now - more & more people have gotten used to that painfully awkward meeting (which is a good thing!)

However, this means that deeper concerns are appearing quicker than ever in the dating-sphere, and there’s a good reason why…

“A high credit score can help predict whether someone is
trustworthy”, say’s Quentin Fottrell of MarketWatch.3

That “trust” ties back into every single factor of a long term relationship - from fidelity to finances…

About 30% of women and 20% of men say they won't marry a person with a low credit score, according to a new survey from FreeCreditScore.com that polled 1,000 adults.4

Still, that doesn’t mean your Credit Score is a good icebreaker!

As Robin Frankel reminds readers of Bankrate.com, “only 6 percent of respondents say it’s something that should be shared within the first few dates.”5

But at the end of the day, knowing that financial institutions trust your judgement can go a long way with someone who is essentially a stranger.

TAKE AWAY: Even if your score isn’t ‘phenomenal’, being upfront with your partner can help create a solid foundation that other factors just can’t.

(TWO) - LONG-TERM THINKING

Again, there is plenty of room for the causal-dating scene on today’s platforms! In fact, some are designed specifically for...intimate encounters only…

But for those swiping-right for something more meaningful, there are big reasons to take Credit Score into account from the outset.

In a joint study, researchers at UCLA, the Brookings Institution and Federal Reserve Board, report that:

“Initial credit scores and match quality predict subsequent credit usage & financial distress, which in turn are correlated with relationship dissolution.” They also wrote. “Credit scores reveal an individual’s relationship skill and level of commitment.” 6

Which is a fancy way of saying - if you don’t have your sh*t together now, how can a new partner expect you to be there in the long-run?

HOWEVER!

As credit expert & debt coach, Beverly Harzog reminds singles,

“[People] can have a good reason for a low score or debt like medical bills or a divorce. It’s important to make the distinction between someone who has a valid reason for their mediocre credit and someone who is irresponsible with their money.” 7

TAKE AWAY: Give everyone the benefit of the doubt, but don’t ignore the obvious. If you’re serious about a future together, you should both be willing to share faults (& STRATEGIES) that can affect the relationship.

(THREE) - ATTRACTION

Our final observation is a little blunt to be sure...but the truth is that bad credit is the exact OPPOSITE of sexy!

And to be clear, that’s not just the observation of users like Madeline…

A recent article by BusinessInsider reported that for 58% of online daters, a good credit score is more attractive than a nice car.

And half of those surveyed prefer a partner to have a good credit score over an impressive job title, with 40% taking a good credit score over a physically fit body.8

So instead of hitting the gym, it may be time to start brushing up on finances!...

Sound crazy?

Well NYC writer, John Schmidt would beg to disagree:

“To keep it simple, I erased every bit of personality and mention of breakfast tacos from my Tinder profile, leaving just my most photogenic pictures and a mention of my ‘very good’ FICO score... [Eventually] I ended up replacing all my photos with one recent screenshot of my credit score. Within hours, 27 people matched with me.”

Some have even taken this to the extreme, like the website creditscoredating.com which factors those 3 little numbers into every match they make!

TAKE AWAY: Taking pride in your finances can be an unexpected bonus when it comes to meeting a new partner. So, if you’re comfortable (& honest!) it might be worth using some space in your profile to highlight this positive attribute!

CONCLUSION:

It’s becoming increasingly obvious that finances are playing a key role in even FORMING a serious relationship.

And whether we like it or not, a Credit Score is one of the most quickly accessible pieces of financial information about ourselves.

It’s like a financial temperature-check…

Now, as we mentioned, there are reasons someone can have bad credit that are completely out of their control!

And whether you’re looking for love or not, there are some pretty obvious tips that pop up on almost every advice article when it comes to improving your credit.

  • Set up automatic payments.
  • Pay down big bills if you can dip into savings.
  • Use one credit-card for essentials & pay it off every month.

Over time, these little changes can make a noticeable difference since.

As Robin Frankel reminds readers,

“The two biggest things that can crush your credit score: late payments and high balances. These factors alone make up 65 percent of your FICO score!” 9

But when you're looking for love, sometimes patience can be hard to come by!

And a lot of people simply don’t have the money to quickly pay off a large debt, with a recent CNBC article reporting that only 41% of Americans could cover a $1000 emergency with existing funds...
